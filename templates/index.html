UV Rays: Types & Impact

Ultraviolet radiation is invisible to the human eye but critically important for solar panel science, material degradation, and energy generation.

UV-C • 100–280 nm
🛡️
UV-C — Completely Blocked by Ozone
UV-C is the most energetic and dangerous ultraviolet radiation. With wavelengths between 100–280 nanometres, it carries enough energy to break DNA strands instantly. Fortunately, Earth's ozone layer (O₃) in the stratosphere absorbs virtually all UV-C before it reaches the surface.

In solar technology, UV-C is used in sterilization lamps and accelerated aging tests for photovoltaic encapsulants to simulate decades of solar exposure in weeks.
0%
reaches Earth's surface (absorbed by ozone)
PHOTON ENERGY
4.4 – 12.4 eV
UV-C photons carry enormous energy — enough to ionize atoms and molecules. This is why UV-C is germicidal and why ozone absorption is so critical for life on Earth.
SOLAR TECH USE
Accelerated UV Testing
Solar panel manufacturers use UV-C chambers to simulate 25 years of outdoor exposure in just weeks. IEC 61215 and IEC 61730 standards require UV durability testing for certified panels.
WAVELENGTH
100 – 280 nm
UV-C wavelengths are shorter than any visible light. For reference, violet visible light starts at ~380 nm — UV-C is nearly 4× shorter, carrying proportionally more energy per photon.
UV-B • 280–315 nm
⚗️
UV-B — Partially Reaches Earth
UV-B is partially absorbed by the ozone layer — about 5% reaches Earth's surface. It's responsible for sunburn, vitamin D synthesis, and the photodegradation of materials like plastics, EVA encapsulants, and backsheets in solar panels.

Solar panel manufacturers must engineer encapsulants and anti-reflective coatings to withstand 25+ years of UV-B exposure without yellowing, delamination, or power loss.
~5%
of UV-B reaches Earth's surface
PANEL DEGRADATION
EVA Yellowing & PID
UV-B causes photo-oxidation of EVA (ethylene-vinyl acetate) encapsulant, leading to yellowing that reduces light transmission and output. Modern panels use UV-stabilised POE or UV-resistant EVA to combat this.
OZONE SENSITIVITY
1% Ozone Loss = 2% More UV-B
Every 1% decrease in stratospheric ozone allows approximately 2% more UV-B to reach Earth's surface. This directly affects solar panel degradation rates in high-altitude or polar installations.
ENERGY
3.9 – 4.4 eV per Photon
UV-B photons have just enough energy to cause chemical changes in organic molecules — the basis of both sunburn and material degradation in solar components.
UV-A • 315–400 nm
🔆
UV-A — The Key Solar Energy Contributor
UV-A is the most abundant UV radiation reaching Earth's surface — about 95% of all UV. Unlike UV-B, it's not significantly absorbed by ozone. UV-A is highly relevant to solar panels because silicon photovoltaic cells can absorb photons down to approximately 300nm, meaning UV-A photons contribute directly to electricity generation.

However, prolonged UV-A exposure also contributes to slow degradation of encapsulants and can cause light-induced degradation (LID) in certain cell types.
~95%
of UV reaching Earth is UV-A (315–400nm)
PV GENERATION
UV-A Powers Solar Cells
Silicon solar cells respond to wavelengths from ~300nm to ~1100nm. UV-A photons (315–400nm) are within this range and generate electron-hole pairs, contributing to electricity output — especially relevant on overcast days when UV-A penetrates clouds better than visible light.
CLOUD PENETRATION
Panels Work on Cloudy Days
UV-A and diffuse visible light penetrate cloud cover. This is why solar panels generate 10–25% of their rated power even on overcast days — the UV component still reaches the cells and generates electricity.
AR COATINGS
AR Coatings Capture UV-A
Modern solar panels use silicon nitride (Si₃N₄) anti-reflective coatings specifically tuned to minimise reflection across 300–1100nm, including the UV-A range, maximising photon absorption and efficiency.
VISIBLE • 400–700 nm
🌈
Visible Light — Primary Solar Power Source
Visible light (400–700nm) carries 46% of total solar energy and is the primary driver of PV electricity generation. Silicon solar cells are most efficient in the 400–900nm range, with peak spectral response around 800–900nm.

The photovoltaic effect works when visible photons — particularly red and near-infrared — excite electrons in silicon from the valence band to the conduction band, creating electrical current.
46%
of solar energy arrives as visible light
BANDGAP PHYSICS
Silicon Bandgap: 1.12 eV
Silicon has a bandgap of 1.12 electron-volts. Photons with energy above 1.12 eV (wavelengths below ~1100nm) can excite electrons. The blue end of visible light has excess energy that becomes heat; red light is near-optimal for silicon cells.
SPECTRAL MISMATCH
Blue Light = Wasted Energy
High-energy blue photons (400–500nm) excite electrons but their excess energy becomes heat rather than electricity — this is called thermalisation loss. Multi-junction solar cells mitigate this by using different materials tuned to different wavelengths.
AM1.5 STANDARD
The AM1.5G Test Standard
All solar panels are rated under the AM1.5G spectrum — sunlight passing through 1.5 atmospheres (38° angle), representing average mid-latitude conditions. This gives 1,000 W/m² for standardised testing.
INFRARED • 700nm–3000nm
🔥
Infrared — Heat & Near-IR Power
Infrared radiation (IR) constitutes ~53% of total solar energy. Near-IR (700–1100nm) is critically important for silicon solar cells — silicon has peak spectral response at 800–950nm, making near-IR its most efficient generation zone.

Mid and Far IR (above 1400nm) is mostly converted to heat in the panel, contributing to cell temperature rise. Every 1°C above 25°C reduces panel output by ~0.4%.
~53%
of solar energy is infrared radiation
TEMPERATURE COEFFICIENT
Heat = Lost Power
Standard silicon panels lose ~0.35–0.45% output per °C above 25°C (STC). A panel at 65°C on a hot summer day loses 16–18% of rated power. Adequate ventilation and bifacial panels help.
BIFACIAL ADVANTAGE
Capturing Albedo IR
Bifacial solar panels capture IR reflected from the ground (albedo). White gravel, snow, or sand can reflect 20–80% of incident light, boosting bifacial panel output by 5–30%.
THERMAL IMAGING
IR Cameras Find Hotspots
Our management platform integrates drone-based infrared thermal imaging to detect hotspot defects, bypass diode failures, and delamination in solar panels — faults invisible to the naked eye but bright in IR cameras.

1
Photon Absorption
Sunlight (photons) strikes the anti-reflective coated tempered glass and passes through to the silicon PV cell. The AR coating reduces reflection from ~30% (bare silicon) to less than 3%, trapping maximum light.
2
Electron Excitation (Photoelectric Effect)
When a photon with energy ≥ 1.12 eV (silicon bandgap) hits the P-N junction, it knocks an electron free from its bond, creating an electron-hole pair. This is the fundamental photovoltaic effect, described by Einstein in 1905 (Nobel Prize 1921).
3
Electric Field Separation
The P-N junction creates a built-in electric field. This field sweeps electrons toward the N-type layer (negative) and holes toward the P-type layer (positive), preventing recombination and driving directional current flow.
4
DC Current Collection
Metal fingers (silver busbars) on the front and rear collect the electrons and route them through external circuits. Multiple cells are connected in series (strings) to build voltage, and strings in parallel to build current.
5
Inverter Conversion (DC → AC)
The string inverter or microinverter converts direct current (DC) from panels to alternating current (AC) at grid frequency (50Hz/60Hz). Modern inverters achieve 97–99% conversion efficiency with MPPT optimisation.

Did You Know?

Solar Science Facts

🌙
NIGHT GENERATION
Panels Work Without Direct Sunlight
Solar panels generate electricity from diffuse light — scattered UV and visible light from clouds, haze, and the sky dome. On a fully overcast day, panels still produce 10–25% of rated output.
❄️
COLD WEATHER
Cold + Sunny = Best Performance
Solar panels are more efficient in cold weather. At 0°C vs 25°C (STC), a panel with -0.4%/°C coefficient produces 10% more power. Snow-covered mountains with high irradiance see some of the highest recorded panel efficiencies in the field.
🔬
QUANTUM PHYSICS
The Photovoltaic Effect Is Quantum
Solar panels operate on quantum mechanics — specifically the photoelectric effect. Einstein's 1905 paper explaining that light behaves as discrete photon packets (quanta) was the theoretical foundation. This is the same paper that won him the 1921 Nobel Prize in Physics!
🌱
ENERGY PAYBACK
Panels Pay Back Energy in 1–4 Years
A solar panel takes 1–4 years to generate the energy used to manufacture it. Over a 25-year life, a panel produces 6–25× the energy invested — one of the best energy returns of any technology.
🔭
SPACE SOLAR
Space Panels Are 30%+ Efficient
Multi-junction solar cells used in satellites and space probes achieve 30–40% efficiency by stacking multiple semiconductor layers, each tuned to a different part of the solar spectrum.
⚡
RECORD EFFICIENCY
Lab Record: 47.6% (Multi-Junction)
The highest solar cell efficiency ever achieved is 47.6% by a concentrator multi-junction cell (NREL, 2023). For commercial silicon panels, the record is 26.7% (Kaneka, IBC). Standard rooftop panels are 20–23%.
